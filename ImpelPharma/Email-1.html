Did you know that patient dissatisfaction with oral medication is vastly underreported? A survey of 3930 US adults with migraine who reported use of oral acute prescription medication showed that 96% of patients said they were dissatisfied with at least one aspect of their migraine treatment.4
 
 

Patients might be dissatisfied with their oral acute treatments for migraine because of:
 
 

Slow onset of action


Autonomic dysfunction is thought to be the shared pathophysiology of migraine and gut comorbidities. Gastrointestinal (GI) disorders can limit what oral medications can achieve by impacting drug absorption.1,4,5

Using Precision Olfactory Delivery (POD®) technology, Trudhesa™ circumvents GI issues by rapidly and consistently delivering dihydroergotamine mesylate (DHE) to the vascular-rich upper nasal space and into systemic circulation.2,6,7

Limited dosing windows 


Many treatments for migraine need to be taken within 60 minutes of migraine attack onset to be most effective. But research shows patients often delay taking treatment for migraine symptoms. 8,9*

Trudhesa can provide relief even when taken late into a migraine attack.10-12†

Lack of consistency and sustained response 


Migraine-associated GI issues may impact complete and consistent absorption of oral treatments.13 Studies of some oral treatments reported the incidence of recurrence varied from 6% to 50%.14,15 In separate studies, sustained pain freedom 24 hours postdose was achieved by about 16% of patients.14,16

Trudhesa provides consistent delivery of proven long-lasting DHE, for sustained relief time after time.2,6,12
