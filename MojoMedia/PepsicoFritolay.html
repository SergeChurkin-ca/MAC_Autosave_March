The Challenge

To improve safety and reliability of electrical infrastructure in both greenfield projects and existing facilities, the following challenges have been identified:

  • • Adherence to long-term corporate standards
  • • Addressing new standards around new products (stainless steel) and best practices for system reliability
  • • Consideration around hygienic principles and installation practices to make infrastructure more cleanable while lasting longer in corrosive environments
  • • Accountability and training on best practices of electrical raceway installations
  • • Continuous improvement by carrying forward lessons learned from project to project and facility to facility

The Solution

Through ongoing engagement, site visits, and collaboration on capital projects, all collaborators involved addressed current standards and installation practices. With exposure to old and new installations, the team started addressing what could be done better to meet the historical challenges head-on. Initiatives taken to tackle these issues include:

  • • Monthly initiative meetings with Principal and Project Engineers
  • • Regular site audits before/during/after projects
  • • Formal installation training and certification on products and best practices for hygienic installations
  • • Specification and standards development
  • • Collaboration with third parties (Engineers, OEMs and Contractors) to ensure consistency throughout projects and facilities
  • • Lessons learned brought improvement to future practices and product design

PVC-Coated

Working on the front end of standards development for this project aided in setting the correct expectation from the beginning. Robroy worked with corporate engineering and the engineering team to detail out the next level of standards and best practices based on Robroy’s product knowledge and several site visits.

This included methods for better transitions to protect and maintain the integrity and life of the electrical raceway system. Robroy was able to:

    Add content to their installer certification program to further address these issues upfront prior to future installations.

    Conduct installer training on-site and through virtual programs to make sure all personnel were familiar with the product and the specific installation requirements.

    Perform an on-site audit after the project was complete for continuous improvement.

The result, as compared to a PVC-coated site audit the prior year, was a raceway system that will maintain increased reliability with uncompromised environmental ratings and increased life and corrosion protection with proper installation methods.
