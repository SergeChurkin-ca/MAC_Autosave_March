NanoCool Shippers
 
As the summer months have now passed and we're moving quickly into winter, the Freenome team has performed an assessment of the need to use NanoCool shippers when transporting FRNM-004 study samples to Freenome during the colder months. The team has determined that effective December 1, 2021, the use of NanoCool shippers will no longer be required when submitting blood samples to Freenome.

Please take note of the following important details as we transition away from the NanoCool shippers over the next few weeks:
  • Please continue to ship all blood samples to Freenome using a NanoCool shipper through November 30, 2021.
  • Sites will have until November 24, 2021 to submit re-supply requests for NanoCool shippers to Freenome. Please take a moment to perform an inventory of NanoCool shippers at your site and place an order for additional shippers as necessary.
  • If your site has additional NanoCool shippers after November 30, 2021, you may still use them to transport blood samples to Freenome, however this will not be required after this date.
  • Although the NanoCool shippers will no longer be required, we are asking that you still continue to use a WarmMark temperature indicator with each sample shipment sent to Freenome. Resupply requests for additional WarmMark indicators may be submitted using the order form located in the Lab Manual.

We will continue to closely monitor the sample stability data throughout the duration of the study. Should the need arise to resume use of the NanoCool shippers, we will be sure to let you know in a future communication.

Friendly Reminders
 

New Guidance on Shipping Samples

  • We have made some revisions to the guidelines for sample collection for the FRNM-004 study. Previously, sites were instructed to ship samples to the Freenome Lab regardless of the number of full blood tubes that were able to be collected during the subject's blood draw. We have since updated this guidance, as follows:
  • A minimum of two (2) fully-filled blood collection tubes (> 8ml) must be collected in order to ship the sample to Freenome. If two full tubes are unable to be collected, discard the samples, and schedule the subject for a redraw.
  • Any used kits not shipped to Freenome should still be accounted for as "used" on the kit accountability form. Additionally, the failed blood collection attempt must also be documented in the EDC.
  • Please review the latest version of the Lab Manual (v7.0) for more information.
 

Investigator Site File and Monitoring Visit Preparation

  • Please ensure that your Investigator Site File is up-to-date and ready in preparation for any upcoming Interim Monitoring Visits (IMVs) and eventual Close-Out Visits (COVs).
  • Please ensure that source documents/data are readily available for the Clinical Research Associates (CRAs) during the IMVs.
 

WarmMark Temperature Indicators

  • Please ensure temperature indicators are activated prior to shipment and adhered to the exterior of the specimen shipping bag.
 
 
FAQs
 
Why are samples failing? We compiled data from last month’s sample failures, and here’s what we found:
What constitutes a blood sample failure?
  • 4 or more non-viable blood collection tubes (BCT) per patient:
    • Blood collection tubes with blood volume that is <8 mL each
    • Clotting of the blood specimen
    • Hemolysis of the blood specimen
  • Blood samples received at the Freenome Lab that are more than 7 days past the blood
    • collection date (i.e., blood sample stability window)
  • Damaged (if blood exposed outside of BCTs)
  • BCTs with missing subject ID and date of collection
  • Smeared BCT labels (illegible writing)
  • Subject IDs and/or accession numbers on all BCTs differs from the requisition form
  • Subject ID and/or accession numbers differs within BCTs and/or req form
  • Date of collection on the req form does not match DOC of at least 2 BCTs
  • Blank Requisition Forms received
What are ways to prevent Subject ID mismatches between the Requisition Form and BCT labels?
  • Implement a quality-control workflow before samples are shipped out
  • Rubber-band the req to the outside of the biohazard bag containing the tubes
