Opportunity to improve CRC screening

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the most preventable cancer but least prevented form of cancer*. Early detection of CRC through screening can improve health outcomes and lower costs, but with so many patients in need of screening and limited capacity for outreach and colonoscopies, more efficient and effective use of limited resources is crucial to improve health outcomes.

Assessing risk can make a difference

Freenome developed risk prediction models that leverage electronic health record (EHR) or claims data or both to indicate how likely each patient is to develop CRC in the next two years. This enables more targeted use of resources to patients with greatest need.

Use your data and optimize resources

Using their data, and without the need for custom workflows, health plans and providers can bring risk prediction to their organization and utilize outputs from the model to direct the right screening resources to the right patients. Organizations can also partner with Freenome on custom, optimized outreach to further drive adherence and improve outcomes.

See how it works in our white paper

In this white paper, we discuss our machine learning approach to training a CRC risk prediction model using a large EHR dataset from the United Kingdom. The model has a two-year predictive horizon and is intended to be used on a population consistent with clinical and coverage guidelines.

Key highlights include:

  • Performance of this model yielded an area under the curve (AUC) of 0.768
  • When applied to real longitudinal patient data, the model correctly identified 80% of CRC patients an average of 3+ years before they were diagnosed
  • Screen-eligible patients representing the top 15% in risk had 50% of the CRC cases
