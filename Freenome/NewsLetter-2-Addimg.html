New Guidance on Shipping Samples
 
We have made some revisions to the guidelines for sample collection for the FRNM-004 study. Previously, sites were instructed to ship samples to the Freenome Lab regardless of the number of full blood tubes that were able to be collected during the subject's blood draw. We have since updated this guidance, as follows:

A minimum of two fully-filled blood collection tubes (> 8ml) must be collected in order to ship the sample to Freenome. If two full tubes are unable to be collected, discard the samples, and schedule the subject for a redraw.
 
 
 
 
 
Any used kits not shipped to Freenome should still be accounted for as "used" on the kit accountability form. Additionally, the failed blood collection attempt must also be documented in the EDC.

Please review the latest version of the Lab Manual (v7.0) for more information.

The latest training video and sample collection checklist can be found on our new Blood Collection Tips & Best Practices webpage.

FAQs
 
Why are samples failing? We compiled data from last month’s sample failures, and here’s what we found:
What constitutes a blood sample failure?
  • 4 or more non-viable blood collection tubes (BCT) per patient:
    • Blood collection tubes with blood volume that is <8 mL each
    • Clotting of the blood specimen
    • Hemolysis of the blood specimen
  • Blood samples received at the Freenome Lab that are more than 7 days past the blood
    • collection date (i.e., blood sample stability window)
  • Damaged (if blood exposed outside of BCTs)
  • BCTs with missing subject ID and date of collection
  • Smeared BCT labels (illegible writing)
  • Subject IDs and/or accession numbers on all BCTs differs from the requisition form
  • Subject ID and/or accession numbers differs within BCTs and/or req form
  • Date of collection on the req form does not match DOC of at least 2 BCTs
  • Blank Requisition Forms received
What are ways to prevent Subject ID mismatches between the Requisition Form and BCT labels?
  • Implement a quality-control workflow before samples are shipped out
  • Rubber-band the req to the outside of the biohazard bag containing the tubes
What are ways to prevent writing from smudging on the BCTs?
  • Use a ballpoint pen.
Are we supposed to use just the Nanocool shippers to send the blood samples from now on? If yes, should we discard the old kits?
  • The NanoCool shippers are now the preferred shipping container to use when transporting collected blood samples to the Freenome Lab. We advise that you use the NanoCool shippers if you have them available. If you do not have them available, it's acceptable to use the original kits, but you should now begin to use the WarmMark temperature indicators with each sample sent to Freenome, regardless of what shipper you use.
We haven't received the WarmMark Temperature indicators yet. Should we continue to send the blood samples in the NanoCool shippers regardless?
  • You should have received the WarmMark indicators in an envelope along with the NanoCool shippers. Please double-check the contents of the shipment. Several boxes may have been sent to your site; please be sure to check all boxes for the envelope. If you did not receive them, please contact frnm004_therapak@freenome.com and the Freenome team will make sure that we send the appropriate materials to your site.
Even if we are using the NanoCool Shippers, do we still use the requisition form, the absorbent tube holder and the gel pack from the old kits
  • Yes, the requisition form, absorbent tube holder, and gel pack from the original kits will still need to be included with the NanoCool shippers. The revised lab manual contains detailed instructions on exactly how the samples should be packed, both with the use of the NanoCool shippers and using the original kits. Please take a moment to review the revised lab manual as you'll find a lot of very helpful information in there.
Can we use the shipping labels that we previously had or do we need to ship the blood samples in the NanoCool shippers with the new shipping labels only?
  • Your site should have received an envelope with the FedEx shipping labels, along with the NanoCool shippers. In the event that you are unable to locate the FedEx shipping labels that were part of the NanoCool shipment, it is acceptable to use the FedEx shipping labels that you previously had, as long as the Freenome address is correct (279 E. Grand Avenue, 5th Floor, South San Francisco CA 94080).
Is it okay to store blood samples at the site at 2-8 degrees C, if we can’t ship them out the same day that they are drawn? If so, how long can they be stored?
  • If samples cannot be shipped to Freenome the same day that they are collected, please store them at 2-8 degrees C until they can be shipped. Samples must be received at Freenome no later than 7 days after collection, so please plan sample collections accordingly, keeping that timeframe in mind.
